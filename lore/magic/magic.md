# Magic — World Lore

This document generalizes the rules of magic drawn from twelve landmark fantasy works. Each rule is supported by examples from the source material.

## Generalized Rules of Magic

### Rule 1: Magic Has a Source

Every magic system draws power from somewhere. The source defines the nature and limits of the magic.

| Category | Examples |
|----------|---------|
| **External material** | Allomancy burns metals; Surgebinding inhales Stormlight from gemstones |
| **Innate / bloodline** | HP wizards are born with magic; WoT channelers are born able to touch the One Power; Tolkien's Maiar/Elves have innate power |
| **Language / symbols** | Earthsea magic uses the Old Speech; AonDor draws Aon symbols to tap the Dor |
| **Energy transfer** | Sympathy redirects existing energy between linked objects |
| **Consciousness / matter** | Dust in *His Dark Materials* is matter becoming self-aware |
| **Spiritual / divine** | LotR magic flows from the being and authority of each creature; JS&MN magic is rooted in the Raven King's fairy pact |

> **General principle:** Magic does not come from nothing. It flows from a defined reservoir — physical, metaphysical, biological, or linguistic — and that reservoir shapes everything about how magic behaves.

---

### Rule 2: Magic Requires a Mechanism

Even when power exists, a practitioner must trigger it through some mechanism: gesture, word, symbol, mental state, or ritual.

- **Wands + incantation:** Harry Potter — swish, flick, and Latin-derived words
- **Hand gestures + incantation + environmental variables:** The Magicians — calculus-like finger choreography adjusted for moon phase, nearby water, etc.
- **Mental binding (Alar):** Sympathy — the caster must genuinely believe two objects are linked
- **Oaths:** Stormlight — Radiants must speak Ideals to unlock deeper powers
- **Drawing symbols:** AonDor — Aons drawn in the air, tied to physical geography
- **Knowing the true name:** Earthsea — speaking a thing's name in the Old Speech grants power over it
- **Submitting or seizing:** WoT — women submit to saidar; men seize and wrestle saidin

> **General principle:** Magic does not activate on its own. A practitioner must perform the correct mechanism — physical, verbal, or mental — and precision matters. Errors degrade or reverse the effect.

---

### Rule 3: Magic Has a Cost

Every magic system exacts a price. The cost may be physical, mental, material, moral, or cosmic.

| Cost type | Examples |
|-----------|---------|
| **Material consumption** | Allomancy consumes metals; the metal supply is the hard limit |
| **Physical exhaustion** | Sympathy — overuse causes "binder's chills" (heat drained from own body); The Magicians — cramped hands, lost voice, mental fatigue |
| **Stormlight depletion** | Surgebinding — Stormlight leaks constantly; holding breath slows loss |
| **Sanity** | WoT — saidin is tainted; all male channelers eventually go mad |
| **Balance / equilibrium** | Earthsea — any magical act risks disturbing the cosmic Equilibrium; the greatest wizards use magic least |
| **Moral corruption** | LotR — the One Ring corrupts; Sauron's power is tied to his domination of others |
| **Multiverse damage** | His Dark Materials — cutting windows between worlds with the Subtle Knife leaks Dust into the void, threatening all life |

> **General principle:** Magic is never free. The cost is the mechanism by which the story creates stakes. A magic system without cost produces no tension.

---

### Rule 4: Not Everyone Can Use Magic

Access to magic is almost always restricted. The restriction defines social hierarchies, conflicts, and character identity.

| Restriction type | Examples |
|-----------------|---------|
| **Genetics / bloodline** | HP — born wizard or Muggle; WoT — some people can channel, most cannot; Allomancy — hereditary, unlocked by trauma ("Snapping") |
| **Bond with a being** | Surgebinding — requires a Nahel bond with a spren |
| **Transformation** | AonDor — only Elantrians (transformed by the Shaod) can use Aons |
| **Learning / discipline** | The Magicians — talent is necessary but not sufficient; years of study required |
| **Species / divine nature** | LotR — Wizards are Maiar (divine), Elves have innate magic; Men and Hobbits cannot wield it directly |
| **Knowledge** | Earthsea — anyone can learn to draw Aons or speak words, but only those who know the true name gain power |
| **Engineered keying** | HPMOR — magic appears to be engineered and keyed to a specific DNA marker by an ancient intelligence |

> **General principle:** Magic is scarce by design. Scarcity creates identity (who is the mage?), conflict (who controls access?), and plot (how does the protagonist qualify?).

---

### Rule 5: Magic Can Be Learned and Improved

Magic is rarely static. Practitioners grow, and the system rewards mastery.

- **Allomancy:** A Mistborn can burn all sixteen metals; a Misting only one — but both can improve control
- **Surgebinding:** Radiants unlock higher tiers by speaking deeper Oaths/Ideals
- **Sympathy:** Strengthening the Alar allows splitting attention across multiple bindings simultaneously
- **The Magicians:** Disciplines are discovered and refined over years; innovation through research or genius
- **WoT:** Channelers grow stronger with practice, and linking multiplies effective power
- **HPMOR:** Harry discovers partial transfiguration by applying physics — magic is open to scientific innovation
- **AonDor:** Modifiers can be added to Aons to refine and extend their effects

> **General principle:** Magic rewards effort, knowledge, and creativity. The most powerful practitioners are not born powerful — they are curious, disciplined, or philosophically committed.

---

### Rule 6: Magic Reflects Morality or Philosophy

In most systems, how magic is used says something about the user's values — and the world punishes misuse.

| System | Moral / philosophical dimension |
|--------|--------------------------------|
| **LotR** | Power-to-dominate = evil; power-as-sub-creation = good. Gandalf wins with Truth, not fireballs |
| **Earthsea** | The greatest magic is restraint. Not-acting is often wiser than acting |
| **His Dark Materials** | Dust = consciousness, love, curiosity. Destroying it to eradicate "original sin" is the real evil |
| **WoT** | The Aes Sedai are bound by oaths — never lie, never make weapons, never harm except in defense |
| **Sympathy** | Causing harm through a sympathetic link (malfeasance) is explicitly forbidden |
| **JS&MN** | Norrell's hoarding of knowledge vs. Strange's openness; both are contrasted with the wild, fairy-rooted magic they fear |
| **HPMOR** | Magic is a phenomenon to investigate, not accept blindly. Rationalism is itself a moral stance |

> **General principle:** Magic systems encode the author's worldview. The rules of magic are often the rules of the story's ethics in disguise.

---

### Rule 7: Magic Has Hard Limits (or Soft Ones — by Design)

Fantasy magic systems fall on a spectrum from **hard** (explicit, consistent rules) to **soft** (mysterious, undefined power).

| Hard magic (rule-bound) | Soft magic (mysterious) |
|------------------------|------------------------|
| Allomancy, Surgebinding, AonDor | LotR, JS&MN |
| Sympathy, WoT One Power | His Dark Materials (partially) |
| HPMOR, The Magicians | Standard Harry Potter (especially later books) |
| Earthsea (partially — True Names have rules, but Naming is mysterious) | |

Brandon Sanderson's first law: *A magic system's ability to solve problems should be proportional to how well the reader understands it.* Soft magic can create wonder; hard magic can create clever plot solutions.

> **General principle:** Every author must decide how much to explain. Hard magic creates puzzle-box plots. Soft magic creates awe and atmosphere. Most great systems blend both.

---

### Rule 8: Magical Objects and Artifacts Extend or Transfer Power

Most worlds include objects that store, amplify, or transfer magical ability beyond what a practitioner can do alone.

- **Metals / metalminds:** Mistborn — angreal and sa'angreal amplify WoT channelers; metalminds store Feruchemical attributes
- **Wands:** HP — a wand chooses its wizard; allegiance can be won; brother-wands cannot duel
- **Honorblades:** Stormlight — grant Surgebinding without a spren bond, at greater Stormlight cost
- **Aon plates / buildings:** AonDor — Aons etched in metal or architecture have persistent effects
- **The One Ring:** LotR — embodies Sauron's power; corrupts all who hold it
- **The Subtle Knife:** HDM — cuts through any material including the fabric between worlds
- **The Alethiometer:** HDM — compass-like truth-device; only Lyra reads it intuitively
- **Angreal / sa'angreal / ter'angreal:** WoT — amplify, store, or channel the One Power in specific ways

> **General principle:** Artifacts democratize (or dangerously concentrate) magical power. They also outlast their creators, becoming historical and political objects.

---

### Rule 9: Magic Has Consequences for the World

Magic does not exist in isolation — it shapes economies, politics, ecology, and history.

- **WoT:** The taint on saidin forces the Aes Sedai to hunt and "gentle" all male channelers — creating centuries of gender-divided politics
- **HPMOR:** The Interdict of Merlin prevents powerful spells from being written down, causing a slow decline of magical knowledge across generations
- **AonDor:** When the Chasm altered the landscape, all Aons became nonfunctional and Elantris fell — magic is geographically anchored
- **His Dark Materials:** Cutting windows between worlds depletes Dust, threatening the consciousness and survival of all beings across the multiverse
- **The Magicians:** Magic puts electronics on the fritz — the school uses Victorian-era technology
- **LotR:** The fading of the Elves is tied to the weakening of their magical artifacts as the age of Men rises
- **Earthsea:** True names change over time, requiring mages to constantly update their spells

> **General principle:** Magic has systemic effects. It is not a neutral tool — it reshapes civilizations, ecosystems, and history over time.

---

## Summary: The Nine Laws of Magic

1. **Magic has a source** — power flows from a defined reservoir
2. **Magic requires a mechanism** — it must be triggered with precision
3. **Magic has a cost** — nothing is free; cost creates stakes
4. **Magic is not universal** — access is restricted by birth, bond, or learning
5. **Magic can be improved** — mastery rewards curiosity and discipline
6. **Magic reflects morality** — how you use it reveals who you are
7. **Magic exists on a hard/soft spectrum** — by design, not accident
8. **Magical artifacts extend power** — and carry their own consequences
9. **Magic shapes the world** — politically, ecologically, and historically
